Differences Between Speed, Velocity, and Acceleration

Hello, my name is Brad and I am 28 years old. I am the proud father of four children and proud husband to a beautiful wife. I currently work as an Advanced Systems Engineer in Information Security for Walmart Associates, Inc. Most of my projects revolve around different applications of machine learning. I am enrolled in the bachelors program for computer science with a focus in cybersecurity.

Something I have to do recently is work from the office two days a week (yay). The drive is about 15 miles. I start from a standstill in the driveway. I accelerate backward to exit the driveway and again forward to begin my journey. After some acceleration I achieve a constant speed and usually I put on the cruise control. Speed is scalar value which means it can be quantified by a number. Acceleration is described by a value and a direction (a vector). There is no road which goes straight from my house to the office. The road toward my target (the office) is very curvy so while I may be able to maintain a constant speed, I am not always making progress in a straight line relative to my starting point. For example, during one part of my journey I travel a few miles directly east and then a couple more miles due north. The rate of change of my velocity is my acceleration. Through the corners and hills I am constantly changing acceleration (even if cruise control is on) to maintain a certain speed.
