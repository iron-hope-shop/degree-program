Section 4.4 in our zyBook defines the Central Limit Theorem (CLT). The CLT is the basis for assuming averages and totals follow the normal distribution and underlies many of the tests and results used in data analysis. The CLT states that as the sample size drawn from the population with distribution x becomes larger, the sampling distribution of the means approaches that of a normal distribution. The distribution of 1000 sample means approximates a normal distribution as it resembles a bell curve. The resmblence to the bell curve means that its traits will be comparable to a normal distribution where the majority of values are encompassed in the within one standard deviation, ninety-five percent within the second, and so on.

In step seven we determine the "grand" mean of the distribution of 1,000 sample means. My grand mean is 496.67, extremely close to my original population of 496.53.
