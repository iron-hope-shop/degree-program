One-Way ANOVA

The null hypothesis is that the financial, energy, and technology group means are equal. This is represented by H 0 : μ 1 = μ 2 = μ 3 . The alternative hypothesis is that two groups with unequal means exist, or H a : μ i ≠ μ j ,  for some  i ≠ j . The level of significance is 5%, or α = 0.05 . The test-statistic is F=55.07. And the p-value is 0. Since the p-value is less than the significance level, we reject the null hypothesis. Sufficient statistical evidence exists to support the claim that the mean of the ETFs is different. The boxplot confirms that the means are different, especially for the technology ETF (seen below).
