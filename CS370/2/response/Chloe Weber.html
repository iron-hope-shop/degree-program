Human brains are better at recognizing patterns than AI, especially in regards to visual data. AI can be easily fooled by biased data, or unrelated noise, or insignificant changes that humans may not even notice. It is my theory that this is because humans learn from a young age to process two-dimensional images from their eyes as a three-dimensional space. Because we are always moving and looking at our surroundings, we quickly learn to extrapolate data that is just out of our line of sight, and we recognize objects not as still images with set surroundings from a specific angle, but as three-dimensional geometry. But while humans will have the equivalent of years of video of the same objects from different angles in different lighting, AI only has a relatively smaller set of data with which to form patters. In short, humans see 2-d images as 3-d, while AI does not.
