An apt example of an intelligent system is an autonomous vehicle. These vehicles leverage a variety of technologies such as Lidar, radar sensors, computer vision, GPS, and intricate mapping to navigate their environment. They need to predict and react to unpredictable elements like pedestrians, other drivers, and changing weather conditions. Autonomous vehicles learn from every situation they encounter, improving their performance over time. They are considered intelligent systems because they can comprehend, learn from, predict, and adapt to their environment to meet their objectives.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is the broader concept of machines being able to carry out tasks in a way that we would consider "intelligent". Machine Learning (ML), on the other hand, is a specific subset of AI that involves giving machines access to data and allowing them to learn for themselves. In simpler terms, AI is the objective, and ML is one of the pathways to achieve that objective. AI can be rule-based and doesn't necessarily have to learn anything, while ML, by contrast, learns patterns from data and incrementally improves its predictions over time.

I'm also eager to deepen my knowledge about state-of-the-art techniques, frameworks, and tools in AI and ML, and to better understand their real-world applications.
