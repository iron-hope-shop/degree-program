This is the right mentality to have. Two things that few people in the field recognize are that: you create a product suited for a user not suited for yourself, and that testing is important. So many times someone will try to "improve" an application either functionally or non-functionally which we then have to roll back because it is not what the customer wanted. This extra work could easily be avoided. Testing, and I said this in my other response to Mr. Dang Pham, is a very important and often overlooked stage of SDLC. Adults... with degrees... who have worked in software development for 5+ years... often neglect to do testing. It is important to recognize that some testing is more involved. If your application is very complex with many integrations of outside systems, testing will be very hard. It is ALWAYS more work to to go back and fix something (sometimes when the application is running in production it will cost the business money and you a raise/promo) rather than address it during the testing phase.
