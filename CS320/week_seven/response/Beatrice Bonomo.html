In 2003, a hospital in Grand Rapids, Michigan erroneously pronounced 8,500 of its patients dead. According to the article, a patient software update incorrectly converted patient codes from "discharged" to "expired". This error resulted in patients, insurance companies, and government entities receiving notice of patient deaths, causing a lapse in insurance coverage and social security benefits for thousands of people.
