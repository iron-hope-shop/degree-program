I tend to disagree with this. Sign sign-on (OAuth or SAML) allow users to authenticate via a third party for federated identity. So for example, when I sign into a new app on my phone I can use Google's federation to verify my identity and instead of manually creating an entirely new account, my resources are associated with a pre-existing source of truth. That saves users a significant amount of time and honestly, when properly implemented, do not affect application load time significantly.

Developers outside of any organization's InfoSec are usually blisfully unaware of security best practice.
