This carries over to enterprise work as well but I would advise that while tools such as programming languages and operating systems are good to know, you certainly have to prioritize your focus when approaching a coding project. The basis of a project should primarily be the business need and secondarily the platform on which it stands. I know it is hard to temporarily ignore the wealth of technical knowledge we are being introduced to here at SNHU but regardless of the programming language, functionality is more important. We need to start on simplest terms when approaching problems. With very few exceptions, the performance of high level languages such as java, javascript, python, etc. are quickly converging. This means that while there are traditional applications of certain programming languages, it is more and more possible to do the same implementation in another language. Why is that significant? We have many tools with which to solve the same problems. So I would advise not to stress too much about different technologies and to dive deeper into javascript or python because when you fully understand it, it will be way less stressful and you can make decisions based on functionality. That being said it is important to understand the reason we do what we do and a part of that is understanding code. But that is (in my opinion) one of the later steps of professional development.
