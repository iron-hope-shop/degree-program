Your insights on Virtual Reality (VR) controllers are spot on. Motion sickness is indeed a concern with VR. Developers are exploring ways to reduce this, such as optimizing frame rates and refining movement mechanics. As for safety, designated play areas and boundary systems can help prevent accidents. Augmented Reality (AR) glasses could be an alternative. They overlay digital content onto the real world, reducing motion sickness risks and allowing users to remain aware of their surroundings. However, they might not offer the same immersive experience as VR. While VR has its challenges, its potential is undeniable, especially with backing from giants like Nvidia. AR might be a safer, more accessible alternative, but the choice depends on the application and user preference.
