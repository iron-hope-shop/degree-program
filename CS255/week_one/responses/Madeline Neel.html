This is so true! My team uses JIRA which is a third-party software used, in part, for project management. We use an agile methodology called Scrum rather than the traditional waterfall. This consists of sprint planning meetings where we determine business needs followed by sprints. For us, sprints are two weeks (other than the first sprint of a fiscal year which we call sprint 0 and it lasts only 1 week). We have our projects organized as "epics" within JIRA and have further broken them down into "stories" which are tasks that can be completed in enough time to fit them into a sprint. Usually these stories are bite-sized and we take on a handful apiece per sprint. I tend to believe waterfall has mostly been phased out of mainstream usage based on hearing others speak about their jobs, etc.
